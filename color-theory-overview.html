Color Theory: An Overview

A practical guide to color systems and why they matter in digital, clinical, and educational environments.

The Color Wheel

The color wheel organizes primary, secondary, and tertiary colors and helps designers create balanced relationships between hues.

Color Schemes

  • Monochromatic: variations of one hue.
  • Analogous: neighboring colors on the wheel.
  • Complementary: opposite colors with strong contrast.

RGB vs. CMYK

Digital interfaces use the RGB additive model, while print projects rely on CMYK subtractive color for production.

Color Theory Overview

Understanding Color Theory

A foundational guide to hue, saturation, value, and color harmony.

Color theory blends science and art to explain how humans perceive color, how colors interact, and how they influence emotion. Designers, artists, and marketers rely on these principles to create visually appealing and psychologically impactful compositions.

Key Terms in Color Theory

TermDefinition
HueThe pure form of a color without tint or shade.
SaturationThe intensity or purity of a color.
ValueThe lightness or darkness of a color.
TintA color mixed with white.
ShadeA color mixed with black.
ToneA color mixed with gray.
Primary ColorsRed, blue, yellow.
Secondary ColorsGreen, orange, purple.
Tertiary ColorsMix of a primary and adjacent secondary.
ComplementaryOpposites on the color wheel.
AnalogousNeighbors on the color wheel.
TriadicThree evenly spaced colors.
MonochromaticVariations of a single hue.
Split-ComplementaryBase color + two adjacent to its complement.
Warm ColorsReds, oranges, yellows.
Cool ColorsBlues, greens, purples.
Color HarmonyPleasing arrangement of colors.
Color WheelDiagram showing color relationships.
RGBDigital color model.
CMYKPrint color model.

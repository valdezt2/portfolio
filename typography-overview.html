Typography Overview

Understanding Typography Anatomy

Typography is the structural foundation of written communication.

Typography goes beyond choosing a visually appealing font. Every letterform is carefully designed, and understanding its anatomy helps designers, educators, and communicators create more effective layouts.

These structural guidelines improve readability and support consistent hierarchy across screens, handouts, and presentations.

Structural Guidelines

  • Baseline: The invisible line that aligns characters consistently.
  • X-Height: The height of lowercase letters, which strongly affects readability.
  • Cap Height: The height of uppercase letters measured from the baseline.
  • Ascender Line: The upper limit for letters such as b and d.
  • Descender Line: The lower limit for letters such as g and p.

Stroke Anatomy

  • Stem: The primary vertical or diagonal stroke of a letterform.
  • Arm: A stroke that extends outward without connecting at one end.
  • Loop: A closed or partially closed counter shape.
  • Terminal: The ending of a stroke without a serif.

Serif and Style Details

  • Serif: Small finishing strokes that help guide the eye through text.
  • Bracket: The curved connection between a serif and the main stroke.
